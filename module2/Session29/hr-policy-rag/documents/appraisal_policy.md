# InnoTech Solutions — Performance Appraisal Policy
**Version:** 3.0 | **Effective Date:** April 1, 2025 | **Owner:** HR Department

---

## 1. Purpose

The Performance Appraisal Policy at InnoTech Solutions is designed to provide a structured, transparent, and consistent framework for evaluating employee performance. It aims to align individual contributions with company goals, identify development opportunities, and determine merit-based compensation increments.

---

## 2. Scope

This policy applies to all permanent employees who have completed at least **6 months of service** by the end of the appraisal period. Employees on probation, interns, and contract staff are not covered under this policy.

---

## 3. Appraisal Cycle

InnoTech Solutions follows an **annual appraisal cycle**:

| Milestone                        | Timeline               |
|----------------------------------|------------------------|
| Goal-Setting for the year        | April 1 – April 15     |
| Mid-Year Check-In                | October 1 – October 15 |
| Year-End Self-Assessment Opens   | February 1             |
| Manager Review Period            | February 15 – March 5  |
| Calibration Meetings             | March 6 – March 20     |
| Final Ratings Published          | March 25               |
| Increment Effective Date         | April 1                |

- The **appraisal year** runs from **April 1 to March 31**.
- Employees who join between April 1 and September 30 are eligible for a **full appraisal** in their first cycle.
- Employees who join between October 1 and March 31 receive a **partial review** and are not eligible for an increment in that cycle.

---

## 4. Appraisal Components

Each employee is evaluated on two broad dimensions:

### 4.1 Goal Achievement (60% weightage)

- At the start of the year, employees and managers jointly set **4–6 SMART goals** on the HR Portal.
- Goals are categorised as:
  - **Business Goals** (primary deliverables, KPIs)
  - **Development Goals** (skills, certifications, learning targets)
- At year-end, the employee rates their own achievement, followed by the manager's assessment.

### 4.2 Behavioural Competencies (40% weightage)

Employees are assessed on the following core competencies:

| Competency                  | Description                                                   |
|-----------------------------|---------------------------------------------------------------|
| Ownership & Accountability  | Taking initiative and seeing tasks through to completion      |
| Collaboration               | Working effectively across teams and functions                |
| Communication               | Clarity, frequency, and quality of communication             |
| Innovation                  | Proposing and implementing improvements                       |
| Customer Focus              | Prioritising internal/external customer needs                 |

---

## 5. Rating Scale

InnoTech Solutions uses a **5-point rating scale**:

| Rating | Label                  | Description                                                              |
|--------|------------------------|--------------------------------------------------------------------------|
| 5      | Exceptional            | Consistently exceeds all goals; role model for peers                     |
| 4      | Exceeds Expectations   | Surpasses most goals; demonstrates strong competencies                   |
| 3      | Meets Expectations     | Achieves all core goals; performs competently in role                    |
| 2      | Partially Meets        | Achieves some goals; requires improvement in key areas                   |
| 1      | Does Not Meet          | Fails to achieve most goals; significant performance concern              |

> Ratings of **1 or 2** trigger a mandatory **Performance Improvement Plan (PIP)** to be initiated within 10 working days of rating publication.

---

## 6. Appraisal Process

### Step 1 — Self-Assessment
The employee completes a self-assessment on the HR Portal by **February 14**, rating their own goal achievement and providing evidence or commentary for each goal.

### Step 2 — Manager Review
The manager reviews the self-assessment, rates the employee independently, and schedules a **one-on-one appraisal discussion** between February 15 and March 5.

### Step 3 — Appraisal Discussion
A mandatory face-to-face (or video) discussion is held where the manager:
- Shares feedback on performance and competencies.
- Discusses career aspirations and development areas.
- Aligns on the preliminary rating.

### Step 4 — Calibration
Department heads and HR conduct **calibration sessions** to ensure ratings are consistent and equitable across teams. No rating can be finalised without going through calibration.

### Step 5 — Final Rating & Communication
- Final ratings are published on the HR Portal by **March 25**.
- Employees receive an automated notification with their rating and increment details.
- Employees who disagree with their rating may raise a **formal appeal within 7 days** of publication.

---

## 7. Increment Linkage

Salary increments are directly linked to appraisal ratings. The following bands are indicative and subject to annual review by the compensation committee:

| Rating | Increment Range (% of Basic Salary) |
|--------|--------------------------------------|
| 5      | 20% – 30%                            |
| 4      | 12% – 20%                            |
| 3      | 6% – 12%                             |
| 2      | 0% – 5%                              |
| 1      | 0% (PIP initiated)                   |

- **Promotions** are considered separately from increments and require a rating of 4 or above for two consecutive years, along with a vacancy in the higher band.
- Increments take effect from **April 1** and are reflected in the April payslip.

---

## 8. Promotion Policy

- Promotions are based on performance rating, tenure in current grade, and availability of the next-level role.
- All promotion decisions are finalised during the **calibration phase** and communicated alongside appraisal results.
- An employee is eligible for promotion only if they have spent a **minimum of 2 years** in their current grade.

---

## 9. Appeals Process

- Employees dissatisfied with their final rating may submit an **Appraisal Appeal Form** via the HR Portal within **7 calendar days** of rating publication.
- The appeal is reviewed by a panel comprising the **skip-level manager and an HR Business Partner**.
- A decision is communicated within **15 working days**.
- The panel's decision is final and binding.

---

## 10. Confidentiality

All appraisal data, ratings, and compensation details are strictly confidential. Employees must not discuss their individual ratings or increments with colleagues. Breaches of confidentiality may result in disciplinary action.
